Supermart Homepage

Project overview

Flipkart, India’s leading e-commerce marketplace, launched ‘Flipkart Supermart’, its online grocery store, in metropolitian cities. Supermart is an online grocery service available on Flipkart. Users can explore, build basket and purchase from a huge catalog of products ranging from Staples, packaged food, Personal care and household care and also provides offers and delivery options to the users. Supermart is app in app experience within Flipkart app and has a homepage aimilar to Flipkart homepage.

Flipkart users’ land on Supermart homepage (~80%) from different touchpoints on Flipkart like browse through categories or different banners on Flipkart homepage. Quantitative data suggests that there is a 40% bounce rate and 3.8% conversion rate from GHP. This leads to the hypothesis that users’ are unable to comprehend the widgets on GHP, initiate basket building or find value from current GHP. We also tapped into the data like scroll depth, widget CTRs to come up with our research questions.

The goal of the study was to evaluate the Grocery homepage (GHP) on Flipkart which will help product and business teams to build GHP according to users’ needs amd comprehension.


Role: User Research

Team members: 4 Product Managers, 4 Business Managers and 3 Product Designers.


Research questions

  • What do users think of the GHP?
  • Are users able to comprehend the different widgets and the value provided on GHP?
  • What do they feel about the different widgets?
  • What values are perceived by the users and why?
  • Do users understand the difference between organic & inorganic properties (Category led widgets vs Ads widgets)?
  • Can users successfully navigate from and to GHP components?

Research objectives

Gain an understanding of the following aspects:

  • Understand visceral impressions and their impact on users’ attitudes and behaviours on GHP
  • Validating existing hypotheses wrt GHP.
  • Discover areas that need improvement and arrive at specific needs and pain points wrt GHP.

Recruitment

I discussed the recruitment plan with my stakeholders to ensure that all the team members align with the recruitement criteria. Participants were recruited through a recruiting agency. A recruiting screener was shared with the vendor who provided a list of potential users, sift through the responses, schedule appropriate participants, and manage their compensation for us.

Recruitment criteria

Fair mix of gender [M(12), F(12)], age group [20-45 years], working professionals (18) and homemakers (6), family size.

  • Cohort 1 : Flipkart Grocery shoppers (including Flipkart plus members)
  • Cohort 2 : Grocery shoppers on other apps
  • Cohort 3 : New Grocery shoppers on Flipkart

Methodology

I conducted one-on-one interviews with 24 participants. I used a combination of exercises were used to uncover research objectives.

  • First impressions : This method is to elicit conversation about GHP in assessing layout considerations and information design.
  • Unaided recall : We asked the participants to describe what they recollect seeing on the grocery homepage, based on their memory.
  • Quick exposure memory test : We showed the participants our GHP for a very brief moment, then take it away. Then, we asked the participants to recall what they remember about the user interface from that brief exposure.

Participants participating in first impression task

  • Validate content comprehension, relevance and expectation from GHP : Participants went through the grocery hompage and discussed content comprehension and relevance with respect to different widgets.
  • Cards activities : This was to assess the comprehensibility of the offer callouts.

We provided users with visual cards(cutouts), each one depicting a component of the GHP and ask them to build an GHP by re-ordering the widgets as per their preference

  • Co-creation task - to identify the preference of widgets on GHP.

Data synthesis

After every session, we had debriefing sessions with the product managers, Business folks and Designers to reflect on each session. I did my analysis with the help of atlas.ai tool by tagging individual observations and quotations with appropriate codes, to facilitate the discovery of significant themes. Some of the insights that we distilled from our analysis and synthesis are:

Insight 1

Rs 1 recall was strong but users expected to see variety of products in this deal.

“Here, fortune oil, I will not use only at home. So if they are giving at 1 rupee also, it’s not of any use for me. Instead of that, if I get the chance of taking the brand which I like, it would have been still more interesting for me.” - P5, Bangalore

Implication : How might we provide more value to the current users?

Insight 2

Grocery homepage perceived to be lengthy and repetitive (products & offers) which created confusion around offers value.

“I don’t scroll so much to be very honest. This is the first time I have scrolled the page and all these things are creating confusion and it is strected so much. It is complicating as everything is already there above.” - P3, Delhi

Implication : How might we design page which increases certainty?

Insight 3

Some offers titles (Flash sale, trending offers, spotlight etc) did not match users’ mental model and hence created puzzlement.

Implication : How might we display offers according to the mental model to minimise confusion?

Insight 4

Flipkart Plus members receive no benefit of free delivery

“Flipkart me points leke membership lete hai na usse free delivery hoti hai. Woh bhi le rakhi hai tab bhi mujhe extra paisa dena pada delivery ke liye. Usme kya hota hai aapko delivery free dete hai, lekin woh bhi leke koi faayda nahi hai, woh mujhse delivery charges lete hi hai.” - P15, Delhi

Implication : How might we utilise plus coins in Supermart and provide benefit to plus members?

Insight 5

Combination of all offer types (Upto, range, Rs vs % off, etc) within a widget increases confusion in offer comprehension and selection provided

Implication : How might we provide clear distinction between offer types to exhibit significance?
